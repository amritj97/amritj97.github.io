What I learned at CPPIB

Global Corporate Communications Intern, Summer 2018

During the summer of 2019, I started my first internship within the corporate and strategic communications area at Canada Pension Plan Investment Board (CPPIB). I have always been excited about communication across and outside of large organizations and believe that there are a lot of variables that go into effective communication. I worked in the Public Affairs and Communications team, which is responsible for media relations, investment communications and engaging with Canadians (the contributors to the CPP fund), in order to build the global brand of CPPIB.

Since this was my first internship in the field, I was excited to learn a lot and make the most of my internship. Here are some of the lessons I learned during my internship:

Important of Engagement

Although all Canadians who are employed and are over the age of 18 are mandated to contribute to the CPP fund, CPPIB is still heavily involved in engaging with all Canadians. This is in contrast to some companies who do not see value in actively engaging with its users as they believe customers will continue to come to them due to their prices or quality. It was interesting to see CPPIB care about the work that they were doing for Canadians and their passion for everyone to see it. Throughout my time, I was heavily involved with how the company engaged with Canadians through mediums such as social media (Twitter and Facebook) and more traditional media interviews, like press conferences.

The Value of Branding

Working at Canada Pension Plan Investment Board (CPPIB) was an interesting experience as the company finds itself in an interesting position: it is a crown corporation and is accountable to parliament but it is managed independently and only operates within arm’s length of the Canadian government. However, many Canadians believe that the organization is managed by the Canadian government and that investment decisions are made with a political agenda in mind. With this, CPPIB does a lot of work in branding to try and change the negative perception some Canadians have about the organization. Whenever the company made a decision, they made sure it aligned with their brand and the image they wanted Canadians to see.

When it comes to an organization’s branding, there’s a lot more to it than the logo. It’s the way that the organization communicates, both visually and in the written medium, to the intended audience. I learned a lot about the immediate impacts that a brand can have with the consumer base.

Be Willing to Learn

Coming from a business background, there were some aspects of the job I had to learn while I was there. For example, instead of analyzing an investment from a finance standpoint, I would have to look at it through a reputation risk lens and see if it aligned with the company’s mandate. I took the time during my internship to learn about the communications and marketing field and took on initiatives to learn about changes in the industry.

I learned that there’s incredible value in asking questions, especially as an intern. I had the ability to navigate through the culture at CPPIB by asking questions that helped me understand how CPPIB assesses reputation risk with numbers, how to go about writing great press releases and what metrics to look at in social media management. All of these skills comes down to asking great questions.

Networking and Teamwork

One of the things I loved about working at CPPIB was the diverse backgrounds that everyone had. While many had a communications background, others came from the digital space, business and law. It was great working on this team as I believe that everyone’s differentiated experience brought different perspectives to many projects and helped create a stronger team.
